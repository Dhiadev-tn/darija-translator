## Session 3 — Elderly man from Siliana (neighborhood cafe)
- **Collected:** June 11, 2026
- **Place:** cafe in my neighborhood; speaker originally from Siliana
- **Speaker:** elderly man, raised in Siliana (rural northwest)
- **Consent:** verbal — I explained the project and that I was recording; he agreed
- **Recording:** audio recorded with his knowledge — kept PRIVATE, backed up outside this repo, never committed
- **Pairs:** 18 — source `community-siliana`: 1 in `greetings` (regional variant "nharek tayeb"),
  7 in `memories` (childhood, parents' attitude toward education), 10 in `rural_life` (NEW category)
- **Context/notes:** he described the Siliana dialect (different tones, "nharek tayeb" instead of
  "sbe7 el 5ir"), the region ("3roubiya" — mountainous countryside, farming as the main work),
  his childhood (school mornings, football evenings), and how parents of his generation dismissed
  education — his explanation for why many rural Tunisians didn't reach their goals.
  Notable pair: "enti bech twali mou3alem" is sarcastic (literally "you'll become a teacher",
  meaning you never will) — translated with an explicit sarcasm marker in English since a
  literal translation would invert the meaning. Good paper example of pragmatics in
  field-collected vs scraped data.
